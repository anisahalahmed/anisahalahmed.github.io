Project 1

Project overview

Describe your application and its purpose

The application is a promotional website for Hobbes Studios, a dynamic game development company. The primary purpose of this website is to showcase and promote their upcoming game, offering potential players a sneak peek into its features, gameplay, storyline, and other unique selling points. In addition to serving as a marketing tool, the website aims to foster a community of enthusiasts eagerly anticipating the game's release.

Intended users of the website

The intended users encompass a broad spectrum

  • Hardcore gamers and casual gamers interested in Hobbes Studios' releases.
  • Retailers and distributors looking to partner with Hobbes Studios.
  • Fans of the game genre that the upcoming game falls under.

Overview of the content of the website

  • Home: A captivating introduction to the upcoming game, with high-quality graphics and a teaser trailer.
  • About the Game: In-depth details about the game, such as its storyline, gameplay mechanics, features, and development insights.
  • Media Gallery: Screenshots, gameplay clips, and wallpapers for users to view and download.
  • News & Updates: Regular updates about the game's development progress, announcements, and event participations.
  • Community Forums or discussion boards for users to discuss, speculate, and share their excitement.
  • Pre-order and Merchandise: Information on how and where to pre-order the game and purchase related merchandise.
  • Contact: A page for users to reach out to Hobbes Studios with inquiries or feedback.

Page Design

Main

  • Data entry: No.
  • Data validations: No.
  • Page elements: Buttons, hyperlinks.
  • Actions: Navigation to other sections/pages.
Gallery
  • Purpose of the page: Showcase visual media associated with the game, like screenshots, artwork, and videos.
  • Audience/Users of page: Gamers, fans, bloggers, journalists.
  • Content of the page: Image thumbnails, video clips, and descriptions or captions.
  • Data entry: No.
  • Data validations: No.
  • Page elements: Hyperlinks.
  • Actions: Enlarging images, playing videos.
Blogs
  • Purpose of the page: Share insights, updates, and stories related to the game's development and other studio news.
  • Audience/Users of page: Fans, gamers, potential investors, and anyone interested in supporting the game's development.

  • Data entry: No.
  • Data validations: No.
  • Page elements: Buttons, hyperlinks, drop-down menus (for selecting donation tiers or rewards).

Support

Dynamic Functionality on the Website

Interactive Media Viewer
  • Page: Gallery
  • Description: Allows users to click on thumbnails to view high-resolution images or play videos in an overlay. The media can be navigated using arrow keys or swipe gestures.
  • Example URL: Websites like ArtStation or Behance that offer enlarged views of artworks.
Real-time Funding Progress Bar
  • Page: Support
  • Description: Displays the real-time progress of crowdfunding efforts. As contributions come in, the bar updates in real-time to show the amount raised compared to the target goal.
  • Example URL: Crowdfunding platforms like Kickstarter or Indiegogo often have this feature.
Dynamic Blog Search
  • Page: Blogs
  • Description: As users type in the search bar, relevant blog posts appear in real-time based on keywords. It enhances user experience by helping them find content instantly.
  • Example URL: Many news sites or blog platforms, such as Medium, have a similar dynamic search feature.
Interactive Reward Tiers
  • Page: Support
  • Description: As users hover over or click on different funding tiers, they get detailed pop-ups or sidebars that explain the benefits of that particular tier, possibly with animations for emphasis.
  • Example URL: Patreon often offers a detailed view of rewards for specific donation tiers.
Dynamic FAQs
  • Page: Support
  • Description: A list of frequently asked questions where answers expand and collapse dynamically upon clicking. This setup helps users quickly navigate through the list without being overwhelmed.
  • Example URL: Most tech product websites or platforms like Apple Support or Amazon Help have dynamic FAQs.
